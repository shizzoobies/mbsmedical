Common questions about TRT at MBS Medical

Does insurance cover TRT visits?

MBS Medical is a cash-pay practice. We do not accept or bill insurance. Visit fees are transparent and confirmed before you book. There are no surprise charges. Lab costs, when applicable, are separate and disclosed upfront.

Who is a good candidate for evaluation?

Men experiencing symptoms that may be related to low testosterone — such as fatigue, low libido, mood changes, decreased muscle mass, or brain fog — who want a medical evaluation to understand what is going on and what their options are.

How does the evaluation process work?

You complete a short intake and meet with a provider via telehealth to discuss your symptoms and health history. Lab work is a standard part of the process, and your provider will walk you through what the results mean before making any recommendations.

Is TRT automatically prescribed?

No. Whether TRT is appropriate depends entirely on your labs, medical history, and your provider's clinical judgment. Some patients are good candidates; others are not, and your provider will be direct and honest about both outcomes.

What does ongoing monitoring look like?

Patients receiving TRT support are scheduled for regular follow-up visits to review labs, assess how they are responding, and make any needed adjustments to their plan. Ongoing monitoring is not optional — it is part of responsible care.
